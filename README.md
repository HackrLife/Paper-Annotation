# PhD Literature Annotation Framework

A systematic framework for annotating academic papers with integrated analysis and templated parameter mapping, designed for doctoral research in AI governance and human-AI collaboration.

## Annotation Format

Each paper annotation consists of two integrated parts:

### Part 1: Detailed Textual Analysis
In-depth narrative analysis with colored callout boxes for different types of insights:

3. Section-by-Section Annotations
4. Methodological Deep Dive
5. Theoretical Framework Analysis
6. Critical Assessment and Limitations
7. Implications for Research
8. Glossary of Technical Terms
9. Suggested Further Reading

## Part 2: Templated Parameter Mapping
Structured assessment against 9 key parameters:

| # | Parameter | Purpose |
|---|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **Triage** | Paper type, relevance score, bucket classification |
| 2 | **Paper Identity & Positioning** | Bibliographic details, level of operation |
| 3 | **Core Claim (Distilled)** | Conditional claim structure, evidence basis |
| 4 | **System Model** | Pre/post-AI state, transformation vector |
| 5 | **Assumptions** | Technical, organisational, policy/institutional |
| 6 | **Lens Checks** | Human behaviour, incentives, institutional reality |
| 7 | **Comparative Context** | Strengths, weaknesses, literature positioning |
| 8 | **Synthesis & Research Insight** | Pattern recognition, thesis contribution, boundary conditions |
| 9 | **Metadata & Linkages** | Tags, collections, related items, follow-ups |

- **Peripheral**: Tangentially relevant

## Lens Checks

### Human Behaviour Lens
- Human role (in/on/out of the loop)
- Attention and cognitive load
- Trust calibration
- Failure modes (automation bias, algorithm aversion, deskilling, etc.)

## Incentives Lens
- Actor optimisation targets
- Gaming potential
- Goodhart risk
- Incentive alignment

## Institutional Reality Lens
- Enforcement mechanisms
- Cross-boundary coordination
- Regulatory capacity
- Accountability structures

## Usage

### Creating a New Annotation

1. Copy template files to new paper directory
2. Complete Part 1 (Detailed Analysis) first
3. Fill in Part 2 (Parameter Mapping) 
4. Update metadata and linkages
5. Add to index
